Amarnath Temple

The Amarnath Temple is a famous Hindu shrine dedicated to Lord Shiva, located in the Indian state of Jammu and Kashmir. It is situated in the Himalayas at an altitude of about 3,888 meters (12,756 feet) above sea level, and is considered to be one of the holiest sites in Hinduism. The temple is accessible only during a short period of time each year, usually from late June to early August, when the annual Amarnath Yatra (pilgrimage) takes place. The journey to the temple is considered challenging and involves a trek of about 46 km (28.5 miles) from the base camp at Pahalgam or Baltal, with steep climbs and difficult terrain. The highlight of the Amarnath Yatra is the darshan (viewing) of the naturally formed ice Shivling inside the main cave of the temple.It is believed that the Shivlinga represents Lord Shiva in his formless state and is a symbol of his ultimate power. The Amarnath Temple has a rich history. According to Hinduism, it is believed that Lord Shiva revealed the secret of immortality (amrita) to his consort, Mata Parvati, inside the cave at Amarnath. It is also believed that the cave was rediscovered by a Muslim shepherd named Buta Malik in the 15th century. The Amarnath Temple is managed by the Shri Amarnathji Shrine Board, a government body established in 2000 to oversee the management of the temple and the annual yatra. The board is responsible for ensuring the safety and security of the pilgrims, as well as the preservation of the natural environment around the temple.

Opening and Closing time: Temple is open from July to August each year during the Hindu holy month of Shravan.

Best time to visit: During month of July to August when the wheather is relatively mild.

Sanchi Stupa

The Sanchi Stupa is a Buddhist monument located in the town of Sanchi in the Indian state of Madhya Pradesh. It is one of the oldest and most important Buddhist sites in India and is a UNESCO World Heritage site. The Sanchi Stupa was originally built by the Mauryan Emperor Ashoka in the 3rd century BCE as a simple brick structure. Over the centuries, it was expanded and embellished with stone carvings, railings, and gateways by subsequent rulers and Buddhist patrons. The stupa is built on a hill and is surrounded by a large complex of other Buddhist structures, including temples, monasteries, and smaller stupas. The main stupa is a hemispherical dome structure that is approximately 16 meters in diameter and 36 meters in height. The dome is surrounded by a stone railing and four gateways, or toranas, which are elaborately decorated with carvings of Buddhist stories and symbols. The carvings on the Sanchi Stupa are considered to be some of the finest examples of Buddhist art and architecture in India. They depict scenes from the life of the Buddha, stories from Buddhist mythology, and symbolic representations of the Buddha's teachings. The carvings are also notable for their stylistic evolution, which can be traced from the simple, early carvings to the more elaborate and intricate carvings of later periods. Today, the Sanchi Stupa is an important pilgrimage site for Buddhists and a popular tourist attraction. It is considered to be one of the greatest architectural and artistic achievements of ancient India and is a testament to the enduring legacy of Buddhism in the country.

Nalanda University

Nalanda University was an ancient center of learning in Bihar, India, established during the Gupta dynasty around the 5th century CE. It was one of the oldest and most renowned universities in the world, attracting scholars and students from all over India and other parts of Asia. The university was located in the town of Nalanda, which was a major center of Buddhist learning and culture. The campus was spread over a large area and included a library, lecture halls, dormitories, temples, and other buildings. The library was said to have housed hundreds of thousands of manuscripts on a wide range of subjects, including philosophy, religion, astronomy, medicine, and the arts. The faculty at Nalanda University included some of the most prominent scholars of their time, including Buddhist philosophers, mathematicians, astronomers, and linguists. The curriculum was based on the traditional Indian system of education, which emphasized the study of the Vedas and other ancient texts. Nalanda University was known for its high standards of scholarship and research, and its graduates were highly respected in the intellectual and political circles of their time. Many famous figures, including the Chinese scholar and traveler Xuanzang, studied at Nalanda and wrote about their experiences there. The university flourished for several centuries until it was destroyed by Muslim invaders in the 12th century CE. Today, the ruins of Nalanda University are a UNESCO World Heritage site and a symbol of India's rich intellectual and cultural heritage. Efforts are underway to revive the university as a modern center of learning, with the establishment of a new Nalanda University in the same region in 2014.
